A Yogi

I started dabbling in Yoga in 2000, and right away one of the things I loved about it was that I had to adjust my rearview mirror when I got back into my car after class — because I had literally gotten taller

I was inspired to go deeper with my practice after a weekend at Kripalu in the fall of 2008.  When I suddenly and completely unexpectedly found myself in astavakrasana while on retreat in Bali in 2010

Maura Danehey

My Yoga home will always be with the Tranquil Space family, and I am grateful to my many teachers there over the years for all they have shared with me. I have also learned so much from Judy Krupp, Rodney Yee, Colleen Saidman Yee, Mollie Doyle and Jason Crandell.

Off the mat, I can often be found with my kindergartener nephew, on a bike, in hiking boots, looking for the clearest blue water or at Woolly Mammoth, where I am board president.  I am also on the board of Yoga=Union. Occasionally, I wander back into corporate America, where I spent 20 years as an in-house lawyer in the media industry.

I believe the world would be a better place with more Yoga in it.

That’s why I teach Yoga.  I want everyone to feel steadiness and ease, strength and flexibility, equanimity and joy and grace — in our bodies, but also in our hearts, minds and spirits.  If enlightenment feels like spaciousness in the joints (Swami Satchidananda), then Yoga is the light that leads the way.
